DO:
1. Get out as quickly and as safely as possible
Keep in mind that in many cases you may only have a few minutes to safely escape a fire. Many people believe they would have six minutes or more to make their exit. And only eight percent of Americans have acknowledged that their first thought on hearing a fire alarm is to get out.

In a fire, seconds can save lives. Get out as quickly as possible and call the fire department from a safe location. Closing doors on your way out can help prevent the fire from spreading.
2. Tell everyone in the house
When there is a fire, you have to make sure that your family members or anyone in the house in aware of it. I'm sure you don't want to escape without them.

Furthermore, you should remember it's a fire, it takes only a couple of minutes for it to completely vanish your house. So you have to make sure you yell "There's a fire!" and not go up to each one of them calmly.
3. Call Emergency Services
Remember to GET OUT, STAY OUT and CALL 9-1-1 or your local emergency phone number. Yell "Fire!" several times and go outside right away. If you live in a building with elevators, use the stairs. Leave all your things where they are and save yourself.

You don't want to burn alive! So, by the time you manage to get out of your house, you should already call the emergency services. But, If you are stuck inside the house with the doors locked, call 9-1-1 if you're stuck there rigth away!
4. Stay Low
You'll breathe less smoke if you stay close to the ground. Smoke naturally rises, so if there is smoke while you're using your escape route, staying low means you can crawl under most of it. You can drop to the floor and crawl on your hands and knees below the smoke.
5. Pull The Fire Alarm
When you pull a fire alarm, you are alerting the fire department that there is a fire, smoke, or carbon monoxide issue at the facility where the alarm is engaged. You are essentially requesting help from the fire department for a fire-related emergency.

Don't stop or go back to pull the fire alarm! Remember, you have to act fast since fire spreads really quickly. You should pull the fire alarm on your way out.
